📈 Context — Why CA Runs Differently
Higher Wages Drive Higher Productivity and Lower Turnover
California’s higher starting wages attract and retain stronger crew, which is reflected in the portfolio TPPH of 6.91 and an average wage rate of $18.96/hr. Lower turnover means crews are more experienced, more efficient, and better deployed at peak. The hours vs need gap is not a scheduling failure — it reflects crews delivering above the base demand expectation. The management conversation in CA is about protecting crew capacity during peak periods and ensuring the under-need hours don’t create service speed gaps when traffic spikes.
